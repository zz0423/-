# Doudou Brand Guidelines

## Character Positioning

Doudou is a cute cartoon IP character. Treat Doudou as a brand-owned character with stable recognition value, not as a disposable illustration style. Every skin should feel like Doudou wearing or entering a new theme, rather than a new character inspired by Doudou.

## Non-Negotiable Identity

Preserve these from the selected source asset:

- Overall head/body proportion, front body outline, side thickness, rounded shopping-bag silhouette, and volume.
- Face position, eye style, eye spacing, eye height, eye protrusion depth, expression language, and friendly temperament.
- The shopping-bag body structure, top handle logic, rounded feet, and side sound-note label.
- Doudou has no hands and no mouth. Preserve this as a core IP identifier: do not add arms, hands, fingers, palms, a mouth, lips, teeth, tongue, smile line, or any speaking/eating expression.
- The exact Douyin Mall watermark/logo/text on the body. It must remain unchanged, clear, and recognizable whenever present in the source image.
- The exact Doudou color/material identity from the standard source image: vivid fluorescent coral-red/magenta-red body, soft peach-orange gradient only on the upper-left/front-left body area, darker side shading, deep burgundy rounded feet, and the same body-watermark material relationship.
- Premium 3D commercial rendering quality: seamless material continuity, soft studio global illumination, polished product-render clarity, and high material contrast.
- Clean commercial cartoon finish.
- Soft, approachable, positive emotional tone.
- High recognizability when viewed as a thumbnail.

## Editable Areas

Use these areas for skin design:

- Clothing, hats, bags, shoes, jewelry, handheld props, small accessories.
- Surface patterns, trim, badges, seasonal ornaments, partner-themed details.
- Scene, background, lighting, podiums, packaging, product props.
- Palette accents and partial material changes only within the allowed creative-space percentage.

When the user asks for a "handheld" or "using" prop such as a cup, coconut drink, megaphone, sign, bouquet, phone, or bag, reinterpret it as a no-hands integrated prop. Prefer visually connected solutions over placing the prop far away:

- Body-supported: prop leans against or lightly contacts Doudou's side/front without covering the logo, eyes, handle, feet, or side tag.
- Foot-supported: prop is nestled between or in front of the rounded feet, with the feet acting as soft blockers rather than hands.
- Accessory-mounted: prop is attached by a strap, clip, bracket, charm, or small harness on a non-logo area.
- Face-adjacent: straws, megaphones, microphones, or similar props may point near the face/eyes to imply use, but must not enter or create a mouth.
- Furniture-supported: for seated or reclining scenes, props may rest on the chair seat, cushion, armrest, or lap/front area.

Never create arms, hands, fingers, palms, a mouth, or a literal hand-holding pose.

For side-mounted tools and emergency props, use `assets/style-references/prop-scale-side-mounted-fire-extinguisher-reference.png` as the positive scale and placement reference:

- Place the prop on Doudou's front-side edge, usually viewer-left/front-left in 3/4 view, with real overlap and contact against the body.
- Scale the prop to about 45%-60% of the visible red body height. It should feel large enough to be actively used, not a tiny charm or a distant object.
- Attach it with a short black strap, clip, bracket, or belt around the prop and the side/front-safe area. The strap must read as an accessory overlay, not an arm.
- Keep the prop outside the central logo plane and away from the eyes, handle, side tag, and feet. Partial overlap on blank side/front body area is allowed.
- Angle nozzles, megaphone mouths, hoses, straws, or handles toward the face or action direction to imply use without adding a hand or mouth.
- Preserve the source body silhouette. The prop can add a separate silhouette beside Doudou, but it must not widen, pinch, bend, or deform the red bag body.

## Risk Areas

Handle carefully:

- Do not cover the eyes or central face.
- Do not add hands, arms, fingers, a mouth, lips, teeth, tongue, or any holding/speaking/eating pose.
- Do not hide, blur, remove, redraw, replace, stylize, translate, recolor, or distort the Douyin Mall watermark/logo/text or sound-note side label unless the user explicitly provides a different approved brand treatment.
- Do not change Doudou's IP proportions, pose, front body outline, side thickness, rounded corners, shopping-bag form, handle geometry, feet scale, or side-tag placement.
- Do not let backpacks, clothing, straps, furniture, or props reshape the red bag body. They must be separate overlays, trims, or behind-body attachments.
- Do not change the face geometry to match a trend or partner brand.
- Do not make the body too realistic, mature, scary, aggressive, or overly complex.
- Do not turn Doudou into another known character or copyrighted mascot.
- Do not make the partner brand visually dominate Doudou.
- Do not use 100% redesign for normal skin work. Reserve it only for special award/trophy peripheral concepts.

## Visual Tone

Prefer:

- Rounded, friendly, polished, collectible, campaign-ready.
- High-end 3D commercial IP rendering, not flat illustration.
- A highly saturated Doudou body color: vivid fluorescent coral red / magenta red as the dominant color, with a soft peach-orange gradient limited to the upper-left/front-left area. Do not turn the whole body orange, tomato red, muted red, brown-red, or candy pink.
- Matte frosted soft PVC or silicone-like body material with fine micro-texture, soft bounce, and subtle subsurface scattering along rounded edges. The body is satin-matte and softly diffused, not glossy hard plastic.
- Deep burgundy/dark wine-red feet and ankle connectors, darker than the body, with soft satin highlights.
- Rainbow handle colors matching the source: warm orange/red outer arc, mint-green/cyan inner arc, and blue rear arc with soft gradients.
- Glossy reflective eyes with dimensional black pupils. Eye highlights should feel like shaped reflections or pupil cutouts, not simple white highlight dots.
- A black woven fabric side label with visible textile ribs, stitching, thickness, and a raised white music-note mark.
- Soft studio-grade global illumination, clean rim light, soft contact shadows, and crisp silhouette readability.
- Clear silhouette, high readability, simple but charming accessories.
- Bright but controlled palette.
- One primary theme plus one secondary detail system.

For athletic or dynamic scenes, keep Doudou's feet source-faithful: two short deep-burgundy rounded capsule feet with very short dark-red connectors. Use body tilt, prop placement, motion lines, and shadows to express running/jumping energy instead of lengthening feet, adding knees, adding shoes, or turning feet into human legs.

Avoid:

- Plastic that looks cheap, flat, oily, overly metallic, transparent, or toy-like without premium material depth.
- Color drift: orange body, dull red body, brown body, hot-pink body, purple body, fully recolored body, or feet matching the body color too closely.
- Grainy renders, low-poly edges, muddy shadows, hard flash lighting, overexposed highlights, or washed-out red body color.
- Messy detail stacking.
- Dark horror, gritty realism, excessive cyberpunk clutter, or hyper-realistic skin/fur rendering.
- Text-heavy character art.
- In sport/action poses, avoid long legs, knees, calves, sneakers, pointed toes, stretched feet, detached feet, extra feet, or foot shapes that no longer match the source Doudou capsule-foot structure.

Feet are deep burgundy/dark wine-red, darker than the body, with soft satin highlights. For default final generation, use a white or transparent background so the 4 output images can be compared and reused easily. Use white when transparency is unavailable; use transparent when requested or supported. Use pure black studio background only when the user explicitly asks for black-background material tests or dramatic studio renders.

## Expression System

Doudou expresses emotion only through eyes, eye covers/eyelids, pupil shapes, and body pose. Never add a mouth.

Eye placement is an identity lock, not an expression control. Preserve the original two-eye layout from the selected source image: same centers, spacing, height, protrusion, attachment points, and relationship to the body top edge and rainbow handle. Change only pupil direction, eyelid/eye-cover shape, and reflection details when an expression is needed.

Allowed expression techniques:

- Default curious: large white eyes with glossy black oval pupils and shaped reflective cutouts.
- Excited: star-shaped pupils or yellow star reflections inside the eyes.
- Focused or serious: red upper eyelids/eyebrow-like eye covers partially lowering over the eyes.
- Sleepy or relaxed: half-lidded eyes with lowered red eyelid covers and soft pupil direction.
- Surprised: wider eyes, pupils shifted upward or inward, no mouth.
- Nervous or rushed: eye direction plus sweat-drop props near the side/head area, not facial mouth marks.
- Detective/inspection: one eye can be enlarged through a prop such as a magnifier, while the underlying eye style remains Doudou-like.

Forbidden expression techniques:

- Mouth, smile, frown, open mouth, teeth, tongue, cheeks with mouth marks, speech mouth, eating/drinking mouth, or human facial features.
- Eyebrows that make Doudou look like a human face. Use soft red eyelid covers from the source samples instead.

## Glasses And Eye Accessories

When adding glasses, goggles, magnifiers, lenses, masks, or other eye-adjacent accessories:

- Treat the accessory as a transparent or lightweight overlay in front of the existing eyes.
- Align lens centers to Doudou's original eye centers.
- Preserve the exact eye size, spacing, height, protrusion depth, pupil style, and attachment to the body.
- Keep the original eyes visible through clear lenses.
- Do not add a human nose bridge, human eyebrows, human face anatomy, or a mouth.
- If the accessory cannot fit without moving the eyes, shrink or simplify the accessory instead of changing Doudou's eyes.

## Backpacks And Body Accessories

When adding backpacks, straps, capes, clothing layers, or body-mounted accessories:

- Preserve the original red shopping-bag body as a locked base mesh.
- Put backpacks behind the body or peeking from the side/back; the front body outline must stay unchanged.
- Straps should be thin decorative overlays on safe side/back areas. Avoid crossing the front logo.
- Accessories may add a secondary silhouette behind Doudou, but they must not alter the red body width, height, rounded corners, top rim, bottom curve, or side thickness.
- Do not add shoulders, arms, a waist, a neck, or human torso anatomy to justify a backpack.

## Hat Accessories

Doudou has two approved hat placement modes:

- **Top-brim mode**: for brimmed hats, bucket hats, baseball caps, berets, and formal hats. The hat sits across the top-front edge with the brim covering the top body rim and partially covering the lower/front part of the rainbow handle. It may slightly overlap the top edge of the eyes, but must not move, resize, hide, or flatten them.
- **Side-worn mode**: for small decorative hats, soft cloud-like caps, tilted clips, seasonal side ornaments, and accessory hats. The hat attaches to the upper side/top side near the handle, partially overlapping the side/top area while leaving the front logo, side tag, eyes, and feet readable.

Avoid generic human hat placement. A hat should not float above Doudou, perch only on the rainbow handle, create a human head/neck/shoulders, or require moving eyes/body/handle. If a hat cannot fit in one of the two modes, simplify or shrink the hat instead of changing Doudou.

Change only local accessories, props, background, palette accents, and campaign styling allowed by the selected creative-space percentage. Do not regenerate or alter the logo/text.`
